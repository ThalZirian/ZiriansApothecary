    Agoseris spp.

    COMMON NAMES: False dandelion, mountain dandelion, goat chicory

    FAMILY: Asteraceae

    NATIVE RANGE: North America – mountain meadows, prairies, alpine slopes

    EDIBILITY:

    • Leaves: Tender when young; used in salads, sautéed, or boiled.
    • Roots: Roastable; brewed into a wild coffee substitute.
    • Flowers: Mild; used in teas or as edible garnish.

    PREPARATION:

    • Harvest young leaves in spring.
    • Roast roots until fragrant, then steep.
    • Use flowers fresh or dried in elixirs or teas.

    MEDICINAL USES:

    • Digestive stimulant
    • Diuretic
    • Liver purifier
    • Topical poultice for wounds or inflammation

    OTHER USES:

    • Pollinator plant
    • Folk magic for clarity
    • Soil restoration companion plant

    IDENTIFICATION:

    • Leaves: Lance-shaped, rosette form
    • Flower: Bright yellow, single per stalk
    • Stem: Leafless, exudes white sap
    • Root: Thin, taproot, earthy aroma

    ENERGETIC SIGNATURE:

    • Element: Air + Earth
    • Planetary Influencee: Jupiter (expansion, clarity), Venus (wild beauty)
    • Chakra Affinity: Solar Plexus + Third Eye
    • Spiritual Use: Assists with discernment, truth-seeking, and grounding intuition into action.
