    COMMON NAMES: Cleavers, Catchweed, Goosegrass, Stickyweed

    FAMILY: Rubiaceae

    NATIVE RANGE: Temperate regions of North America and Europe â€“ hedgerows, forest edges, disturbed soils

    EDIBILITY:

    • Leaves: Edible when young; used fresh or dried in teas.
    • Stems: Sometimes used to strain liquids (natural filter due to stickiness).
    • Seeds: Roasted and ground as a coffee substitute.

    PREPARATION:

    • Use aerial parts fresh or dried for infusions or tinctures.
    • Roast seeds for use as a mild coffee alternative.
    • Chop finely when fresh for salads or poultices.

    MEDICINAL USES:

    • Lymphatic cleanser
    • Diuretic and urinary tonic
    • Supports skin healing (eczema, acne)
    • Soothes sore throats when gargled

    OTHER USES:

    • Used in bedding historically due to pleasant scent
    • Used in natural dye (red from roots)
    • Traditionally woven into herb bundles for cleansing

    IDENTIFICATION:

    • Leaves: Whorled in circles around stem, narrow and bristly
    • Flower: Tiny, white, star-shaped
    • Stem: Square, sticky/hairy surface
    • Root: Reddish, fibrous, used for dye
