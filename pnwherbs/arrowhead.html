  • Arrowhead

    Sagittaria spp.

    COMMON NAMES: Arrowhead, Duck Potato, Wapato, Katniss

    FAMILY: Alismataceae

    NATIVE RANGE: Freshwater wetlands across North America — lakes, ponds, marshes, and river edges

    EDIBILITY:

    Arrowhead is a nutrient-rich aquatic food source that was a staple for many Indigenous peoples, especially in wetland regions.

    • Seeds: Occasionally harvested, roasted, or ground.
    • Tubers: Starchy, earthy, and high in carbs. Underground and harvested from mud.
    • Young Leaves and Shoots: Tender when young; can be eaten raw or cooked.

    PREPARATION:

    • Tubers: Boil, roast, or bake like small potatoes. Peel after cooking.
    • Seeds: Dry, then grind into meal or roast for snacks.
    • Shoots: Lightly sauté or steam for mild greens.

      • The Kalapuya, Chinook, and Ojibwe peoples harvested Arrowhead extensively, often using canoes or long digging sticks to reach the prized tubers below water.

    MEDICINAL USES:

    Arrowhead was used as a gentle healer, especially for digestive and urinary health.

    Traditional Herbal Applications:

    • Anti-inflammatory: Crushed tubers applied as poultices for wounds, swelling, or irritation.
    • Digestive aid: Consumed to relieve indigestion, constipation, and bloating.
    • Diuretic: Encourages urine flow and clears fluid retention.
    • Galactagogue: Sometimes used to promote breast milk production in nursing mothers.

    OTHER USES:

    • Ecological restoration: Excellent plant for water purification and erosion control in wetland rehabilitation.
    • Wildlife food: Eaten by ducks, muskrats, and other marshland animals.
    • Cultural significance: Used in trade and ceremony, and often featured in tribal stories and myths.

    IDENTIFICATION:

    • Leaves: Distinctive arrowhead shape, either floating or held upright.
    • Flowers: White, three-petaled blooms, often rising above the water.
    • Habitat: Found in shallow freshwater; often rooted in soft mud.
    • Tubers: Attached to long stolons beneath the water surface; brown-skinned, white inside.

    ENERGETIC SIGNATURE:

    • Element: Water + Earth
    • Planetary Influencee: Moon (intuition, flow), Venus (nourishment, gentleness)
    • Chakra Affinity: Root + Sacral
    • Spiritual Use: Balances inner tides, grounds emotion, and supports resilience through softness.
